National Center for Environmental Health

Recommend on Facebook Tweet ShareCompartir

CDC's National Center for Environmental Health (NCEH) plans, directs, and coordinates a national program to maintain and improve the health of the American people by promoting a healthy environment and by preventing premature death and avoidable illness and disability caused by non-infectious, non-occupational environmental and related factors. We are especially committed to safeguarding the health of populations that are particularly vulnerable to certain environmental hazards - children, the elderly, and people with disabilities.

  • Be Prepared to Stay Safe and Healthy in Winter Keep your family safe during winter weather.

  • Radon in the Home Learn the steps you can take to measure and reduce radon levels in your home.

  • Health Impact Assessments Learn how Tracking Network data can inform health impact assessments.

  • Vector Control for Environmental Health Professionals (VCEHP) Register Today for Our New Online Training

  • Wildfire Readiness Stay alert and take action to protect you and your family from wildfire smoke.

Your Health - Your Environment Blog

Protect Yourself and Your Family from Radon
Radon is the second leading cause of lung cancer after cigarette smoking. If you smoke and live in a home with high radon levels, you increase your risk of developing lung cancer. Having your home tested is the only effective way to determine whether you and your family are at risk of high radon exposure.
